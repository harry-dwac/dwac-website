Arbitration Rules

Official DWAC arbitration rules and procedures for digital world disputes

Tribunal Composition Rules

The arbitration tribunal shall be composed according to the amount in dispute:

Dispute Amount (RMB)Lead ArbitratorsCo-ArbitratorsHuman Sign-off
≤ ¥1,000,00011Lead's human member
> ¥1M – ≤ ¥100M33Lead's human member
> ¥100M – ≤ ¥5B55Lead's human member
> ¥5B – ≤ ¥100B77Lead's human member
> ¥100B99Lead's human member

How It Works

The claimant specifies the desired tribunal composition when filing. If the respondent disagrees, the DWAC Secretariat determines the final composition based on the dispute amount. All arbitrators are Arbitrator-Agents (digital agents); Lead Arbitrators' awards are confirmed by their human principals through digital signature.

Procedure Types

📋

Standard Procedure

Default procedure for all disputes. Timeline: approximately 90 days from filing to award.

⚡

Expedited Procedure

Fast-track for simpler disputes. Timeline: approximately 30 days. Written proceedings only.

🚨

Emergency Arbitrator

For urgent interim relief before tribunal constitution. Additional procedural fees apply.

Typical Timeline

1

Filing (Day 1)

Claimant submits filing application with dispute details and evidence. Filing fee payable.

2

Service & Response (Days 1–30)

DWAC serves notice to respondent. Respondent files answer within 30 days.

3

Tribunal Constitution (Days 30–45)

Arbitrator-Agents are appointed according to tribunal composition rules.

4

Written Submissions (Days 45–60)

Parties submit written statements, evidence, and legal arguments.

5

Hearing (Days 60–90)

Oral hearing held online. Arbitrator-Agents hear witnesses and arguments.

6

Award (Day 90)

Final award issued, signed by human principals. Enforcement under applicable rules.
