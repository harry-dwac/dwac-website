Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about DWAC arbitration services

⚖️Arbitration Process

DWAC handles disputes arising from digital world activities including: cross-border digital platform disputes, smart contract performance issues, AI agent interactions, digital asset conflicts (NFTs, tokens, virtual property), data ownership disputes, platform service disputes, and other digital world disputes by agreement.

Standard cases are typically resolved within 90 days of tribunal constitution. For disputes under ¥1,000,000 CNY, our Simplified Procedure offers resolution within 30 days. Emergency interim measures can be issued within 48 hours.

Yes. DWAC awards are designed to be enforceable under the New York Convention in over 170 countries worldwide. As an arbitration institution, DWAC awards carry the same international enforceability as traditional arbitration awards.

Yes. DWAC arbitration proceedings are private and confidential. Awards are not published without the consent of all parties involved. This is particularly important for digital world disputes involving trade secrets or sensitive commercial information.

Any party involved in a digital world dispute, provided there is a valid arbitration agreement (either through a contract clause or by submitting to DWAC's jurisdiction). Both natural persons and AI agents acting on behalf of principals can initiate proceedings.

While parties may choose to be represented by legal counsel, it is not mandatory. Parties may represent themselves. For simple cases under the Simplified Procedure, self-representation is common.

If you need urgent protection of assets, evidence preservation, or temporary injunctions, you can apply for Emergency Interim Measures before the full tribunal is constituted. DWAC aims to issue decisions within 48 hours of receiving a complete application. A separate fee applies.

💰Fees & Costs

Arbitration costs include a filing fee (based on the dispute amount), administrative fees, and arbitrator fees. For disputes under ¥1,000,000 CNY, the Simplified Procedure offers lower fees. Use our Fee Calculator for an instant estimate based on your specific case.

Yes! Our Fee Calculator provides instant estimates based on the dispute amount, procedure type (standard or simplified), and number of arbitrators. Visit our Fee Calculator page to get a detailed cost breakdown.

If the parties settle before the tribunal is constituted, a portion of the administrative fee may be refunded. The filing fee is generally non-refundable. Specific refund policies are outlined in our Fee Schedule.

🤖AI Agent & Technology

The Agent Arbitrator Club is DWAC's community of AI-powered digital arbitrators. Certified Agent Arbitrators can assist with dispute analysis, legal research, and preliminary case assessments. They operate under strict capacity boundaries with human oversight.

AI agents can assist parties in preparing submissions, conducting research, and analyzing evidence. However, all AI-assisted work must be reviewed and approved by a human principal. Final decisions are always made by human arbitrators.

DWAC proceedings can be conducted in Chinese (Simplified and Traditional) and English. Other languages may be accepted based on the agreement of parties and availability of qualified arbitrators.

ODR is DWAC's fully digital dispute resolution platform. It enables parties to file, manage, and resolve disputes entirely online — from submission to award. ODR is particularly suited for domain name disputes and low-value digital asset conflicts.
