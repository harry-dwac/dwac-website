How to Apply for Arbitration

A comprehensive guide to the arbitration process, from filing your application to enforcing the award

1

Submit Arbitration Application

Initiate the arbitration process by submitting a formal application to DWAC. Your application must include all required documents and meet the specified criteria for acceptance.

Required Documents

  • Signed arbitration agreement or contract clause
  • Completed Application Form (Form ARB-01)
  • Statement of claim with supporting evidence
  • Copies of relevant contracts and correspondence
  • Power of attorney (if represented by counsel)

Key Points

  • Ensure the arbitration clause covers the dispute
  • Submit in duplicate plus one copy for each respondent
  • Electronic filing accepted via portal
Processing: 5-10 business days
2

Pay Filing Fees

Upon acceptance of your application, you will receive an invoice for filing fees. Payment must be completed before the case proceeds to the next stage.

Fees Structure

  • Filing fee based on claim amount
  • Arbitrator appointment fee
  • Administrative charges
  • Refundable deposit for hearing costs

Payment Methods

  • Bank transfer (SWIFT/TT)
  • Credit card (Visa, MasterCard)
  • Online payment portal
Due: Within 15 days of invoice
3

Appoint Arbitrators

Arbitrators are appointed according to the rules specified in your arbitration agreement. DWAC maintains a panel of experienced arbitrators from diverse backgrounds.

Appointment Process

  • Party-appointed arbitrators (if applicable)
  • Institution-appointed arbitrators
  • Presiding arbitrator/Sole arbitrator selection
  • Arbitrator independence declaration

Arbitrator Qualifications

  • Minimum 10 years of relevant experience
  • Legal or industry expertise
  • Fluency in arbitration language
Timeline: 10-20 business days
4

Hearings

The arbitral tribunal conducts hearings where parties present their case. Both written submissions and oral arguments are considered.

Hearing Procedures

  • Pre-hearing conference and procedural order
  • Document production phase
  • Written witness statements and expert reports
  • Oral hearings with cross-examination
  • Closing submissions

Venue Options

  • Physical hearing at DWAC premises
  • Virtual/online hearings
  • Hearing at neutral location
Duration: 1-5 days depending on complexity
5

Award Rendering

After considering all evidence and arguments, the tribunal deliberates and issues the final award. The award is final and binding (subject to limited grounds for challenge).

Award Components

  • Summary of facts and issues
  • Reasoning and legal analysis
  • Determination on costs
  • Final decision on claims

Post-Award Options

  • Correction of clerical errors
  • Interpretation of award
  • Challenge proceedings (limited grounds)
Rendering: 30-60 days after hearings
6

Award Enforcement

DWAC awards are enforceable globally under the New York Convention. The winning party may seek enforcement in any of the 170+ signatory states.

Enforcement Process

  • Obtain certified copy of award
  • Apply to local court for recognition
  • Address any challenges to enforcement
  • Execute against debtor's assets

Enforcement Assistance

  • Authentication and certification services
  • Translation services (if needed)
  • Coordination with local counsel
Varies by jurisdiction

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of disputes can be submitted to arbitration?

DWAC handles commercial disputes including contract breaches, partnership disagreements, construction disputes, intellectual property conflicts, and trade matters. The dispute must fall within the scope of your arbitration agreement.

How long does the arbitration process take?

The duration varies based on case complexity. Simple cases may conclude in 6-12 months, while complex disputes may take 18-24 months or longer. Emergency arbitration procedures are available for urgent matters.

What are the costs involved?

Costs include filing fees (scaled to claim amount), arbitrator fees (based on hourly rates or fixed fees), and administrative expenses. DWAC provides fee calculators on our website. Each party typically bears its own legal costs.

Can I use emergency arbitration for urgent relief?

Yes, DWAC offers emergency arbitrator procedures for situations requiring urgent interim relief before the full tribunal is constituted. Applications for emergency relief can be filed at any time.

Is the arbitration award confidential?

Yes, DWAC arbitration proceedings are confidential unless parties agree otherwise or disclosure is required by law. This confidentiality extends to the award, submissions, and all hearing-related materials.
